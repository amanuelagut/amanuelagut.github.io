Agricultural Market Vulnerability in Emerging Economies: A Six-Year Empirical Analysis of Prices, Trade and Biotechnology in Colombia

Angie Manuela Gutierrez-Oviedo · Fundación Universitaria del Área Andina · 2025
Working Paper LaTeX PDF Jupyter Notebook Panel Regression GARCH GitHub
Abstract
This capstone synthesizes six years of empirical research into a submission-ready working paper on agricultural market vulnerability in Colombia. Using Python for econometric analysis (panel regression, GARCH volatility modeling) and LaTeX for academic typesetting, the paper examines patterns of price dynamics, trade competitiveness, and biotechnology adoption that create structural vulnerability in emerging agricultural economies. The full analysis is documented in Jupyter Notebooks and hosted on GitHub for reproducibility. The paper follows academic publication standards: formal literature review, explicit identification strategy, robustness checks, and structured conclusions with policy implications.
1.
Research Question

What patterns of agricultural market vulnerability emerge across six years when price dynamics, trade flows, and biotechnology adoption are examined together for Colombia?

Each year of the portfolio addressed one dimension of agricultural market vulnerability: price volatility (2020, 2022), trade structure (2021, 2023), biotechnology (2023, 2024), and econometric forecasting (2024). The 2025 capstone integrates these threads into a unified analytical framework that examines how they interact and compound to create systemic vulnerability in Colombia's agricultural sector.

3.
Methodology

The working paper applies three interconnected econometric methods, each representing the culmination of skills developed across the portfolio.

Step 1 — Panel regression with fixed effects. Country and time fixed effects control for unobserved heterogeneity in a panel regression that estimates the determinants of agricultural price volatility across Latin American countries. Instrumental variables address potential endogeneity in biotechnology adoption.

Step 2 — GARCH volatility modeling. GARCH(1,1) models estimated for each major commodity capture conditional volatility dynamics — the tendency for volatility clustering (high-volatility periods following high-volatility periods) that the 2020 CV analysis could only describe statically.

Step 3 — Structural break and regime analysis. Formal Chow tests and regime-switching specifications test whether the relationships estimated in the panel model changed structurally during the COVID-19 pandemic and the 2022 commodity price surge.

Step 4 — LaTeX academic typesetting. The paper is formatted in LaTeX following standard economics working paper conventions: abstract, JEL codes, literature review, data section, methodology, results, robustness, and conclusions. BibTeX manages references.

4.
Key Finding

The integrated analysis reveals that agricultural market vulnerability in Colombia is multi-dimensional and self-reinforcing.

Key Finding
Panel regression results indicate that exchange rate volatility, trade partner concentration, and low biotechnology adoption are individually significant predictors of agricultural price vulnerability, and their effects are compounding — countries with high exposure on all three dimensions show disproportionately higher price volatility than the sum of individual effects would predict. GARCH models confirm that Colombian agricultural commodity prices exhibit significant volatility clustering, with shocks persisting for 3–5 months. Structural break analysis confirms regime changes during COVID-19 and the 2022 Ukraine crisis.

The compounding nature of vulnerability dimensions suggests that single-factor policy interventions (e.g., exchange rate stabilization alone) will have limited effectiveness. A comprehensive strategy addressing trade diversification, technology adoption, and macroeconomic stability simultaneously is needed to reduce agricultural market vulnerability.

5.
What I Learned

Writing a submission-ready working paper — with formal identification strategy, robustness checks, and LaTeX typesetting — represented the integration of six years of progressive skill building into a single academic output. The process taught me that research communication is as rigorous a discipline as research analysis.

GARCH volatility modeling formalized the intuition about volatility clustering that I first observed in fríjol prices at Corabastos in 2020. What started as a descriptive observation ('prices seem more volatile during crises') is now captured in a mathematically precise conditional variance model.

GitHub-based reproducibility — version control, documented notebooks, and structured repositories — closes the loop on the reproducibility principle that began with separated spreadsheet tabs in 2020. The entire analysis can be independently verified by any researcher with a Python environment.

6.
Limitation
Scope & Method Limitation
The working paper is a single-author effort without institutional peer review. While the methods are sound and the data is publicly verifiable, the analysis would benefit from external feedback from agricultural economists with field expertise in the specific markets studied. Additionally, the panel includes only 10 countries, which limits the statistical power of cross-country regressions. The visiting scholar position sought by the author would provide the collaborative environment needed to address these limitations.

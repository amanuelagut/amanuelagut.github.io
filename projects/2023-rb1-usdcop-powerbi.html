Research Brief 1 2023 · 2020 – 2023 · Power BI

USD/COP Exchange Rate Risk and Agricultural Import Costs: A Power BI Visual Analysis

Angie Manuela Gutierrez-Oviedo · Fundación Universitaria del Área Andina · 2023
FRED Banco de la República Power BI Exchange Rate Import Costs
Abstract
This brief revisits the USD/COP-to-agricultural-import-cost relationship first analyzed in the 2020 portfolio, now using Power BI's interactive visualization capabilities to communicate the findings to a non-technical audience. The analysis demonstrates how Power BI's slicers, conditional formatting, and dynamic chart titles can transform statistical analysis into an accessible narrative about exchange rate risk and its impact on food costs.
1.
Research Question

How can Power BI effectively communicate the relationship between USD/COP volatility and agricultural import cost dynamics to a non-technical audience?

The 2020 analysis established the exchange rate-import cost relationship statistically. This brief asks a different question: can the same insight be communicated visually to decision-makers who do not interpret regression output?

2.
Data & Sources

The same FRED and Banco de la República data sources from 2020 are used, extended through 2023.

3.
Methodology

The brief focuses on visualization design rather than statistical computation.

Step 1 — Dual-axis time series. USD/COP and import costs plotted on synchronized dual axes with conditional formatting that highlights periods of high volatility in red.

Step 2 — Scatter plot with trend line. An interactive scatter plot with Power BI's built-in trend line shows the exchange rate-import cost relationship, with slicer-based period filtering.

Step 3 — Executive narrative page. A summary page with key metrics (correlation, average pass-through, maximum divergence) presented as KPI cards with plain-language annotations.

4.
Key Finding

The visual analysis demonstrates that Power BI can make complex statistical relationships accessible without requiring audience familiarity with regression output.

Key Finding
The interactive scatter plot with trend line makes the positive exchange rate-import cost relationship immediately intuitive. The conditional formatting on the time-series chart draws attention to high-volatility periods (March 2020, October 2022) that coincide with major economic events. Stakeholder feedback indicated that the visual format was more actionable than the 2020 spreadsheet-based analysis.

The communication format matters as much as the analytical rigor. The same finding — that exchange rate depreciation increases import costs — reaches different audiences depending on how it is presented.

5.
What I Learned

Visualization as communication — not just as analysis output — was the central lesson. Choosing between a line chart and a scatter plot is a communication decision about what relationship to emphasize.

Power BI's conditional formatting and dynamic titles enable data-driven storytelling where the visual itself adapts to the data, reducing the need for manual annotation.

The exercise of revisiting a 2020 analysis with a 2023 tool demonstrated the value of progressive methodology — the same question produces deeper insight when approached with more sophisticated instruments.

6.
Limitation
Scope Limitation
The brief prioritizes visual communication over analytical depth. The underlying statistical relationship is assumed from the 2020 and 2022 analyses rather than re-estimated. A more rigorous approach would embed Power BI's R or Python visual integration to compute and display regression results within the dashboard.

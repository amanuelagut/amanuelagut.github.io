Research Brief 2 2023 · 2015 – 2023 · Power BI

Agricultural Biotechnology Adoption in Latin America: Crop Yield Patterns 2015–2023

Angie Manuela Gutierrez-Oviedo · Fundación Universitaria del Área Andina · 2023
FAO ISAAA Power BI Maps Trends Biotechnology
Abstract
This brief uses Power BI's mapping and trend visualization capabilities to explore the relationship between biotech seed adoption rates and crop yield performance across Latin American countries between 2015 and 2023. Using FAO production data and ISAAA biotechnology adoption statistics, the analysis creates interactive geographic maps and trend charts that reveal spatial patterns in technology adoption and their association with agricultural productivity.
1.
Research Question

What visual patterns emerge in the relationship between biotech seed adoption and crop yield performance across Latin American countries between 2015 and 2023?

Agricultural biotechnology — particularly GM seeds for soybeans, corn, and cotton — has been adopted at different rates across Latin American countries. Understanding whether higher adoption correlates with better yield performance is a foundational question for agricultural development policy.

2.
Data & Sources

The analysis combines global agricultural production data with biotechnology-specific adoption metrics.

Variable Source Frequency Period Access
Crop yields (tons/hectare)FAO FAOSTATAnnual2015–2023fao.org/faostat
Biotech crop area (million hectares)ISAAAAnnual2015–2023isaaa.org

Eight Latin American countries with significant biotech crop production are included: Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay, Uruguay, Bolivia, Colombia, Mexico, and Honduras. Three crops are analyzed: soybeans, corn, and cotton.

3.
Methodology

The brief uses Power BI's geographic and temporal visualization capabilities.

Step 1 — Choropleth maps. Country-level biotech adoption rates visualized as filled maps with color gradients, showing the geographic distribution of technology adoption across the region.

Step 2 — Yield trend lines. Small multiple line charts show yield trajectories for each country-crop combination, with visual comparison between high-adoption and low-adoption countries.

Step 3 — Adoption-yield scatter. An interactive scatter plot with biotech area on the x-axis and average yield on the y-axis, with country labels and time-period slicers.

4.
Key Finding

The visual analysis reveals a general positive association between biotech adoption and yield levels, with important country-specific variation.

Key Finding
Countries with higher biotech adoption rates (Argentina, Brazil, Paraguay) consistently show higher average yields for soybeans and corn than lower-adoption countries. However, the relationship is not purely linear — Uruguay achieves high yields with moderate adoption, suggesting that complementary factors (soil quality, management practices, infrastructure) mediate the biotechnology-yield relationship. Colombia, with limited biotech adoption, shows yields below the regional frontier, particularly for corn.

The visual patterns suggest that biotechnology is a necessary but not sufficient condition for yield improvement. The cross-country comparison motivates the formal econometric analysis conducted in the 2024 and 2025 portfolios.

5.
What I Learned

Building choropleth maps in Power BI introduced geographic visualization as an analytical tool — using spatial patterns to generate hypotheses about regional differences in technology adoption.

Small multiples — showing the same chart type for multiple categories side by side — proved more effective for cross-country comparison than overlapping lines on a single chart. This design principle carries through all subsequent visualization work.

The biotechnology-yield relationship became a core research interest that develops into formal panel regression analysis in the 2025 portfolio, demonstrating how visual exploration can motivate rigorous empirical investigation.

6.
Limitation
Method Limitation
Power BI visualizations show correlation patterns but cannot establish causation. The positive association between biotech adoption and yields may reflect confounding factors (countries that adopt biotech may also have better infrastructure, education, and capital access). The 2025 RB1 addresses this with panel regression controlling for these confounders.

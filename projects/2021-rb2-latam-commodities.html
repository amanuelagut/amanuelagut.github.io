Research Brief 2 2021 · 2018 – 2021 · SQL

Top-Growing Agricultural Commodities in Latin America 2018–2021

Angie Manuela Gutierrez-Oviedo · Fundación Universitaria del Área Andina · 2021
UN Comtrade SQL JOIN Commodity Growth Latin America
Abstract
This brief identifies which agricultural commodities showed the highest trade volume growth across Latin America between 2018 and 2021. Using SQL JOIN queries on UN Comtrade data, the analysis crosses commodity classification tables with bilateral trade flow tables to rank products by growth rate, revealing structural shifts in the region's agricultural trade composition driven by changing global demand patterns and pandemic-era supply chain adjustments.
1.
Research Question

Which agricultural commodities showed the highest trade volume growth in Latin America between 2018 and 2021, and what drove this growth?

Understanding which products are growing fastest helps identify where competitive opportunities and risks lie for Colombian agricultural exporters. The 2018–2021 window captures both pre-pandemic trends and pandemic-induced shifts in global food demand.

2.
Data & Sources

Trade flow data is joined with commodity classification tables to enable product-level growth analysis.

Agricultural trade flows (USD, kg)UN ComtradeAnnual2018–2021comtrade.un.org
HS commodity descriptionsUN Comtrade / WCO——comtrade.un.org

The analysis covers all HS 4-digit agricultural products traded by the 10 Latin American countries in the study scope, filtered for products with non-zero trade in both 2018 and 2021 to enable valid growth computation.

3.
Methodology

JOIN queries combine trade flow data with product classifications, followed by growth rate computations.

Step 1 — JOIN trade flows with product names. INNER JOIN connects the numeric HS codes in the flow table with descriptive product names in the classification table, making results human-readable.

Step 2 — Aggregate by product across region. GROUP BY product code computes total Latin American export and import values per product per year, creating a regional-level product portfolio.

Step 3 — Growth rate ranking. 2021/2018 growth ratios are computed for each product, ranked by descending growth, and filtered for products with minimum trade thresholds to exclude statistical noise from tiny trade flows.

4.
Key Finding

The commodity growth analysis reveals clear winners and losers in Latin America's evolving agricultural trade portfolio.

Key Finding
Oilseeds (soybeans, sunflower seeds) and processed food products showed the highest growth rates over the period. Traditional staples like wheat and rice showed stable but slow growth. The most notable pandemic-era shift was increased demand for storable protein sources (pulses, legumes), consistent with global food security concerns during 2020–2021.

The growth in processed products suggests that value-added agriculture is expanding faster than primary commodity trade in the region — a trend with implications for Colombia's export strategy and food industry investment.

5.
What I Learned

SQL JOINs as analytical building blocks — this project cemented the understanding that joining tables is not just a database operation but an analytical method for enriching data with context (adding product names, classification hierarchies, partner metadata).

Computing growth rates with minimum thresholds taught me to handle the small-sample problem: a product going from $1,000 to $10,000 has 900% growth but is economically irrelevant. Filtering by absolute value before ranking is essential for meaningful results.

The regional aggregation perspective — looking at Latin America as a whole rather than just Colombia — expanded my analytical frame from national to regional, a shift that carries through the 2023 Power BI dashboards and 2025 panel regressions.

6.
Limitation
Method Limitation
Simple growth rates do not account for base effects or price vs. volume decomposition. A product whose trade value grew 50% might have experienced a 50% price increase with zero volume change. Separating price and volume effects requires additional data on unit values, which is partially available in Comtrade but not utilized in this brief.

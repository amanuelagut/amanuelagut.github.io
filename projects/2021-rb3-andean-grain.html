Research Brief 3 2021 · 2017 – 2021 · SQL

Colombia's Share in Andean Grain Imports 2017–2021

Angie Manuela Gutierrez-Oviedo · Fundación Universitaria del Área Andina · 2021
UN Comtrade Subqueries Andean Region Grain Imports Market Share
Abstract
This brief examines Colombia's share of grain imports within the Andean region (Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia) over 2017–2021, using SQL subqueries to compute market shares and detect structural shifts. The analysis tests whether Colombia's relative position as a grain importer changed during a period marked by currency fluctuations, pandemic disruptions, and evolving regional trade agreements.
1.
Research Question

How did Colombia's share in regional Andean grain imports change between 2017 and 2021, and what structural factors explain this trend?

The Andean countries share grain import dependency (particularly corn, wheat, and soybeans from Argentina, Brazil, and the US). Comparing Colombia's import patterns with its neighbors reveals whether country-specific factors (exchange rate, trade policy) or region-wide forces drive import dynamics.

2.
Data & Sources

Grain import data for four Andean countries is extracted from UN Comtrade using HS codes for cereals (10), oilseeds (12), and milling products (11).

Variable Source Frequency Period Access
Grain imports — Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia (USD, kg)UN ComtradeAnnual2017–2021comtrade.un.org

The four Andean CAN members are selected for their geographic and economic comparability. Venezuela is excluded due to incomplete Comtrade reporting during this period.

3.
Methodology

Subqueries compute regional totals that serve as denominators for country-level market share calculations.

Step 1 — Regional total subquery. A subquery computes total Andean grain import value per year, which becomes the denominator for share calculations in the outer query.

Step 2 — Country share computation. Each country's annual grain imports are divided by the regional total, producing a time series of import market shares that sum to 100% per year.

Step 3 — Decomposition by grain type. The share analysis is repeated separately for corn, wheat, and soybeans to test whether composition effects drive aggregate share changes.

4.
Key Finding

The Andean grain import analysis reveals that Colombia's share of regional imports remained relatively stable, with product-specific variation.

Key Finding
Colombia accounted for a consistent share of total Andean grain imports throughout the period, making it the largest importer in the group by absolute value. Peru showed the fastest import growth rate, gradually increasing its share. Corn imports showed the most volatility in shares across countries, driven by exchange rate differentials and domestic production fluctuations in each country.

The stability of Colombia's aggregate share masks compositional shifts — corn imports grew while wheat imports declined relative to the region. This suggests that Colombia's grain import profile is slowly changing, potentially reflecting shifts in animal feed demand (corn) and domestic wheat production capacity.

5.
What I Learned

Subqueries for derived denominators — computing a regional total within a subquery and using it in the outer query's denominator taught me the power of nested computation. This pattern reappears in Python as groupby().transform() operations in pandas.

The regional benchmarking perspective — measuring Colombia not in absolute terms but relative to comparable neighbors — introduced the comparative analysis framework that is central to the 2025 panel regression work.

Understanding composition effects — that an aggregate share can be stable while underlying product shares change dramatically — is a foundational analytical insight that guards against misleading aggregate statistics.

6.
Limitation
Data Limitation
The exclusion of Venezuela (due to incomplete reporting) removes the historically largest Andean grain importer from the analysis. Additionally, the analysis uses value-based shares rather than volume-based shares, which means that price fluctuations can shift shares without any change in physical quantities traded.

Agricultural Price Risk in Colombia 2018–2022: Volatility, Correlation and Price Transmission

Angie Manuela Gutierrez-Oviedo · Fundación Universitaria del Área Andina · 2022
FRED SIPSA-DANE Banco de la República Regression Time Series t-test Volatility
Abstract
This capstone integrates volatility measurement, correlation analysis, and price transmission modeling into a unified framework for assessing agricultural price risk in Colombian grain markets over 2018–2022. Using Excel's Analysis ToolPak, LINEST regression functions, and formal hypothesis testing (t-tests, F-tests), the analysis builds a multi-method risk assessment that connects international commodity benchmarks with domestic wholesale prices. The capstone represents the transition from descriptive to inferential statistics in the portfolio.
1.
Research Question

What integrated picture of agricultural price risk emerges when volatility, correlation, and price transmission are applied simultaneously to Colombian grain markets?

The 2020 portfolio documented price shocks descriptively; the 2021 portfolio mapped trade networks at scale. This capstone asks: can we move from description to inference — testing hypotheses about price relationships using formal statistical methods, still within the Excel environment?

3.
Methodology

The capstone applies three interconnected analytical methods, each representing a skill advancement from the 2020 baseline.

Step 1 — Rolling volatility computation. 12-month rolling standard deviations and coefficients of variation computed for each domestic and international price series. The rolling window approach — as opposed to static period comparison in 2020 — captures how volatility evolves over time.

Step 2 — Correlation matrix with hypothesis testing. Pearson correlation coefficients computed across all price series, with t-tests for statistical significance at the 5% level. The Analysis ToolPak automates the p-value computation that was done manually in 2020.

Step 3 — Price transmission regression with lags. Multiple regression models estimate the pass-through from international to domestic prices, with lagged independent variables (1, 2, 3 months) and the exchange rate as a control. F-tests evaluate overall model significance; individual t-tests evaluate each coefficient.

Step 4 — Structural break detection. Chow-test logic (splitting the sample at March 2020 and comparing regression coefficients) tests whether the price transmission relationship changed structurally during the pandemic.

4.
Key Finding

The multi-method risk assessment reveals that Colombian grain markets are significantly connected to international benchmarks, but with important nuances in transmission speed and completeness.

Key Finding
International corn prices (CBOT) show statistically significant transmission to Colombian wholesale corn prices with a 1–2 month lag and approximately 60–70% pass-through. The exchange rate amplifies this transmission: a 10% peso depreciation increases the effective domestic price impact by an additional 3–5 percentage points. The Chow test indicates a structural break in March 2020, with post-pandemic transmission becoming faster but more volatile.

The incomplete pass-through and lagged transmission suggest that domestic intermediaries and inventory management partially buffer international shocks — but this buffering capacity diminishes during extreme episodes like COVID-19 and the 2022 grain price surge.

5.
What I Learned

The transition from descriptive to inferential statistics — using t-tests, F-tests, and p-values to make formal claims about significance — represents the single largest methodological advancement in the portfolio up to this point. The discipline of stating null hypotheses and testing them against data is foundational for all subsequent work.

Excel's Analysis ToolPak as a statistical environment taught me that serious analysis is possible within constrained tools. The experience of computing regression diagnostics manually (R², adjusted R², standard errors) built understanding of what Python's statsmodels automates in 2024.

The concept of structural breaks — that a regression relationship can change at a specific point in time — connects the statistical work to real economic events. This insight is formalized in the 2025 capstone's panel regression framework.

6.
Limitation
Method Limitation
The regression models assume linearity and do not formally test for non-stationarity in the time series (unit root tests are not available in Excel's ToolPak). Additionally, the Chow test implementation is manual and does not account for unknown break dates. The 2024 Python portfolio addresses these limitations with ADF tests and ARIMA modeling.
